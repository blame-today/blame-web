what counts as a "blame"

one tap on an entity. that's it. no comment field, no thread, no photo, no reply. just blame: trump / blame: my landlord / blame: AI / blame: cilantro — whatever resonates today.

one vote per device per day, on the UTC clock. then you wait until midnight and you can vote again on whatever has resonated by then. the app shows a countdown when you've voted; the countdown is anchored to the public ledger, not just a local timer.

what's blameable publicly

anything in our curated pool — about 34,000 entries drawn from public figures, brands, countries, concepts, and newsworthy entities pulled from the day's headlines. the pool is the union of:

  • wikipedia top-pageview entries — filtered for vulgarity, disambig pages, and obvious-PII patterns.
  • daily news entities — extracted by the worker each morning from ~10 free news sources (yahoo, npr, the guardian, hacker news, reddit subs, etc.) via a small LLM step.
  • prediction-market signal — top markets from polymarket + kalshi, used as an attention proxy.
  • hand-curated overrides — abbreviations (BJP → Bharatiya Janata Party), display-label corrections (Drake → the rapper, not the disambig page), known-bad entity ids dropped.

if it's in the pool, your vote lands on the public ledger. if it's not in the pool, your vote stays on your device. the gate is at the pool, not at the vote.

what stays private (on your device)

freeform text that doesn't match a pool entry — your landlord by name, a coworker, the gas station that overcharged you — gets saved locally on your iphone only. it does NOT hit our worker, it does NOT get signed to nostr, it does NOT appear on anyone else's leaderboard. it sits in your private vote history on the device. you still see the 24-hour cooldown on it, same as a public vote — the cooldown applies to the user action, not the public surface.

this is the "private individuals" carve-out: we don't want to be a platform for naming-and-shaming people who didn't sign up for it. so we let you express the blame, but the public record stays pool-only. the journey to this design is on why → what we wanted vs built.

what we DON'T do

a quick map of the negative space — things people expect from "a social app on your phone" that this deliberately isn't:

  • no logins
  • no accounts
  • no email collection
  • no profile pictures
  • no follows / friends
  • no comments
  • no replies
  • no reactions
  • no DMs
  • no notifications
  • no algorithm
  • no "for you" page
  • no recommendations
  • no story features
  • no live streams
  • no upvote/downvote
  • no shadow-banning
  • no UGC moderation queue
  • no ad SDKs
  • no analytics SDKs
  • no IDFA / advertising ids
  • no third-party trackers

we're a calculator pointed at a daily question, not a social network. one tap per device per day, ranked by simple count, then the app goes away. that's the whole feature surface.

why this isn't UGC

under Apple's App Store Guideline 1.2, a user-generated-content app needs in-app reporting, a 24-hour response SLA, a moderation queue. that's a real obligation we can't responsibly staff as a hobby project.

because all public votes are constrained to entries from a pre-existing pool we curate, the app doesn't function as a UGC platform under that guideline. you can't post freeform text to the public surface. the moderation we do is at pool entry, not at votes-after-the-fact. that's a job we can actually do.

freeform input that doesn't match a pool entry stays on your device. you can type whatever you want in there; we never see it. it's your private journal of who you'd blame, never leaving the device.

the property we want

anonymous + permanent + uncensorable. we (the people running this) can't tell you who voted for what — the worker only sees a sha256 hash of your device id. we can't unpublish a vote that's already on nostr — the protocol holds it on relays we don't control. we can't pull a name from the leaderboard because someone got upset — the math runs on public events anyone can replay.

what we CAN do is decide what's eligible for the public ledger (the pool). that's where our moderation lives — entry into the pool, not censorship of votes once cast. cache is opinion; protocol is record.
