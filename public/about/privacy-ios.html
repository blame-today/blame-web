privacy — the iOS app

no accounts. no tracking. votes are pseudonymous.
this is the privacy policy for the blame.today iOS app. (the blame.today website has a different architecture and its own policy at privacy →.) it follows the structure Apple uses for its App Privacy Details framework and the disclosure requirements of GDPR Article 13. the content is plain-English honest: nearly every line is a "no, we don't" because the app is built around not collecting things.
no account, no sign-up.
no analytics, no ads, no trackers.
your votes are pseudonymous — and deletable.

tl;dr

  • we collect no personal data. no name, no email, no account, no phone, no location, no contacts, no analytics SDK, no third-party tracker, no ads.
  • votes are pseudonymous. they're stored in our database keyed by an opaque Apple-generated iCloud identifier — never your name, email, or Apple ID. we cannot map it to a person.
  • votes are deletable. email us and we'll delete the votes under your identifier. deleting the app clears everything on-device.
  • you can't type anything. votable topics come from the day's news headlines. no user-authored text is ever shown to other users.
  • the AI summaries run on your device. nothing you read or vote on is sent to any AI service, ours or anyone's.

who's responsible

blame.today is a single-person project. the operator (the "data controller" in GDPR terms) is Roy Ashbrook. reach us at hello@blame.today for any privacy question, request, or correction.

what leaves your device when you vote

fieldvalue
topic id + labelthe news topic you voted on (e.g. mondays / "Mondays")
day + countthe UTC date and your running +1/−1 count for that topic that day
opaque iCloud idCloudKit attaches the opaque user record id of your iCloud account to records you create. it's an Apple-generated identifier; we never receive your name, email, or Apple ID.

votes go to our CloudKit database (Apple infrastructure, operated by us). "public database" is Apple's term for the shared container: votes are readable by signed-in users of the app and by our tally job, and the aggregate counts (the leaderboard) are readable by everyone. votes are pseudonymous-public: keyed by the opaque id, never a name.

what reaches the leaderboard

there is no typing in the app. votable topics come from the day's news headlines, extracted on your device. the public leaderboard only shows topics our service has verified against current news plus a denylist — topic names shown to other users are generated server-side from news data, never from any user's input. there is no messaging, no profiles, no images.

third parties

the systems the app talks to, end-to-end:

third partywhat they see
Apple CloudKit / iCloud the vote records above, stored in our container on Apple's infrastructure under Apple's privacy policy. your device authenticates with your iCloud session; Apple does not give us your identity.
Wikipedia REST API topic-detail pages fetch the topic's Wikipedia summary — the same request a browser makes for any article. no identifier attached beyond a normal HTTP request.
news sources (CNN Lite, Fox, brutalist.report) the app normally reads a pre-built headline manifest from our CloudKit container. if that's unavailable it falls back to fetching the sources directly — ordinary article-list requests, same as a browser.
Apple Intelligence (Foundation Models) nothing — the "why the blame" summaries run entirely on your device on supported hardware. no text is sent to any server, ours or Apple's.

our own Cloudflare worker builds the news manifest and computes the leaderboard tallies server-side — the app never talks to it. we don't share data with anyone else. no ad networks, no analytics services, no data brokers.

your rights — and what they translate to here

standard rightwhat it means for the app
access (right to know what data we have) the votes under your opaque iCloud id — which the app already shows you (the MINE tab).
rectification (correction) nothing personal to correct. board corrections work by voting.
erasure ("right to be forgotten") email hello@blame.today and we'll delete the votes under your id. deleting the app clears all on-device data.
portability your votes are visible in-app; ask and we'll export the rows under your id.
object / withdraw consent stop using the app. no processing of personal data continues without you.
complain to a supervisory authority your local data-protection authority. in the EU, find yours at edpb.europa.eu.

children

the app isn't designed for children under 13 and we don't knowingly collect data from them. the App Store age rating reflects this. parents who discover their child is using the app can contact us and we'll delete the votes under that iCloud id.
