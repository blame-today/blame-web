blame.today for iOS — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 10, 2026

This is the privacy policy for the blame.today iOS app. The short version: no accounts, no sign-up, no analytics, no ads, no trackers. Your votes are pseudonymous, and unlike most of the internet, we can actually delete them if you ask.

Who's responsible

blame.today is a single-person project. The operator (the "data controller" in GDPR terms) is Roy Ashbrook. Reach us at hello@blame.today for any privacy question, request, or correction.

What we collect

Two things, both tied to app functionality and nothing else:

DataDetails
Your votes (user content) A +1/−1 on a news topic, with the topic name and the date. That's the product. There is no free text anywhere in the app — no posts, no comments, no messages, no photos.
An opaque iCloud identifier Apple's CloudKit attaches an opaque, Apple-generated user record id to records you create. We never receive your name, email, or Apple ID, and we cannot map the identifier to a person. It exists so the app can show you your own votes.

What we do not collect: name, email, phone, address, location, contacts, health data, financial data, browsing history, search history, purchases, usage analytics, crash diagnostics from our code, or an advertising identifier. There is no analytics SDK and no third-party SDK of any kind in the app.

Where votes live and who can see them

Votes are stored in our CloudKit database (Apple infrastructure, operated by us). Votes are pseudonymous-public: readable by signed-in users of the app and by our tally job, keyed by the opaque identifier, never a name. The aggregate counts (the leaderboard) are readable by everyone. The leaderboard only shows topic names generated server-side from current news — never any user's input.

The AI summaries

The "why the blame" summaries in topic detail run entirely on your device using Apple's on-device models (on supported hardware). No text you read or vote on is sent to any AI service — ours, Apple's, or anyone's.

Third parties the app talks to

ServiceWhat it sees
Apple CloudKit / iCloud The vote records described above, stored in our container under Apple's privacy policy. Your device authenticates with your iCloud session; Apple does not give us your identity.
Wikipedia Topic-detail pages fetch the topic's Wikipedia summary — the same request a browser makes for any article, with no identifier attached.
News sources (CNN Lite, Fox, brutalist.report) The app normally reads a pre-built headline list from our own database. If that's unavailable it falls back to fetching the sources directly — ordinary article-list requests, same as a browser.

That's the whole list. No ad networks, no analytics services, no data brokers, and we don't share data with anyone.

Retention and deletion

  • Votes and leaderboard tallies stay in our database while we operate the service — and are deletable on request: email hello@blame.today and we'll delete the votes under your identifier.
  • On-device caches (headlines, Wikipedia summaries, AI summaries) live on your device until you delete the app.

Children

The app isn't designed for children under 13 and we don't knowingly collect data from them. Parents who discover their child is using the app can contact us and we'll delete the votes under that identifier.

Changes to this policy

If we change this policy materially, every revision is visible in public version control at github.com/blame-today/blame-today.github.io. The date at the top always reflects the current revision.

Contact

hello@blame.today — for GDPR requests, deletion requests, or anything else. It's a hobby project; we read everything and respond as fast as a hobby allows.
