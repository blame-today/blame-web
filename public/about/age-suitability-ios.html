blame.today for iOS — Age Suitability

Last updated: June 10, 2026

blame.today shows today's news headlines and lets you vote on who (or what) you blame. This page is the common-sense version of what that means for age suitability.

The content is the news

The topics and headlines in the app come from current news coverage (mainstream outlets, refreshed through the day). We don't write the news and we don't soften it. That means anything genuinely in the headlines can appear: war, crime, politics, disasters, public figures, and the occasional celebrity nonsense. On a rough news day, the app reflects a rough news day — the same judgment you'd apply to handing someone the day's newspaper applies here.

What keeps it bounded

  • Headlines come from mainstream news sources, not social media or anonymous posts.
  • Topics only appear on the board after being checked against current news coverage plus a denylist we maintain.
  • There is no way to type anything. No user-authored text is ever shown to anyone — the only user action is voting.
  • No chat, no messaging, no profiles, no images from users.
  • No in-app web browser. Headlines link out to the publisher in your regular browser.
  • No ads, no purchases, no gambling mechanics.

Our recommendation

The App Store rating reflects news content: infrequent and mild references to real-world violence and mature themes, because that's what news is. We'd put it this way: if you'd let someone read the front page, the app is fine. It isn't designed for children under 13, and the voting humor ("who do you blame today?") assumes the reader gets that it's satire.
