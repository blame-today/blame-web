this page is the privacy policy for both blame.today products: the website (the firehose at blame.today) and the iOS app. it follows the structure Apple uses for its App Privacy Details framework and the disclosure requirements of GDPR Article 13, so a reader familiar with either standard knows what to look for. the content underneath is plain-English honest: nearly every line is a "no, we don't" because both architectures are built around not collecting things.
we don't track you.
no accounts, no analytics, no ads.
your votes are public by design.

tl;dr

  • we collect no personal data. no name, no email, no account, no phone, no location, no contacts, no analytics SDK, no third-party tracker, no ads.
  • website votes are anonymous. every vote is signed with a fresh throwaway key generated in your browser and used once. there is no stable identifier at all.
  • website votes are published to public nostr relays we don't operate. that publish is permanent, by design. we can't delete it and neither can anyone else.
  • iOS app votes are pseudonymous. they're stored in our database keyed by an opaque Apple-generated iCloud id — never your name, email, or Apple ID. unlike the website, we can delete these on request.
  • nothing private should reach the public record. the website filters what you type before publishing (contact-info shapes, profanity, gibberish). the iOS app has no typing at all — topics come from the day's news.

who's responsible

blame.today is a single-person project. the operator (the "data controller" in GDPR terms) is Roy Ashbrook. reach us at hello@blame.today for any privacy question, request, or correction.

iOS votes go to our CloudKit public database. "public database" is Apple's term for the shared container: votes are readable by signed-in users of the app and by our tally job, and the aggregate counts (tallies) are readable by everyone. votes are pseudonymous-public: keyed by the opaque id, never a name.

about "permanent" (website only)

once a website vote is published to a public nostr relay, we cannot remove it. we don't own the relays. we have no admin call. the protocol doesn't have a delete operation.

practically: as long as any relay we publish to keeps the event, it's accessible. this is a deliberate property of the website's architecture — see why →.

the iOS app is different: its votes live in a database we operate, so deletion there is possible — email us.

what reaches the public record

because website publication is permanent, we constrain what gets published rather than try to delete after:

  • website: your vote text is checked in your browser before anything is sent. contact-info shapes (email addresses, long digit runs like phone/SSN/card numbers), profanity (including spaced/disguised variants), and keyboard-mash are rejected — they never leave your device.
  • iOS app: there is no typing. votable topics come from the day's news headlines, and the public leaderboard only shows topics our service has verified against current news. a topic that isn't in the news doesn't surface, no matter how it got into the database.

children

the products aren't designed for children under 13 and we don't knowingly collect data from them. the App Store age rating reflects this. parents who discover their child is using the app can contact us and we'll delete the votes under that iCloud id — though nothing already published to public nostr relays (website votes) can be removed.
