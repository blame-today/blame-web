this page is the privacy policy. it follows the structure Apple uses for its App Privacy Details framework and the disclosure requirements of GDPR Article 13, so a reader familiar with either standard knows what to look for. the content underneath is plain-English honest: nearly every line is a "no, we don't" because the project's architecture is built around not collecting things.
we don't track you.
your vote is public by design.
once on the public ledger, it's there forever.

tl;dr

  • we collect no personal data. no name, no email, no IP retained, no apple id, no phone, no location, no contacts, no analytics, no third-party tracker.
  • your device id is a random local-only number, hashed before our server sees it. we can't link it back to you.
  • your vote target is published to public nostr relays we don't operate. that publish is permanent. by design.
  • we can't delete your vote from the public ledger. nobody can. that's the architecture.
  • if a target looks like private information, we don't publish it. it stays on your device only.

what gets published — and where

the "user content" category covers one specific thing: the target you blame. when you tap VOTE, the following is what leaves your phone:

fieldvalue
target_idthe canonical id of the entity (e.g. donald-trump) — derived from the label you typed
target_labelthe display string (e.g. Donald Trump)
day_buckettoday's UTC date (e.g. 2026-05-18)
SHA-256(public_key)opaque per-install hash used only for "one vote per day per install"
signatureBIP-340 Schnorr signature over the above, generated locally with a key that never leaves your device

this is published to seven public nostr relays we don't operate and to our cloudflare worker (a fast cache of the public-ledger data). the canonical record is on the relays.

the published event is public by the protocol's design — anyone can subscribe to a public nostr relay and read every event in real time. that's how the "anyone can verify the leaderboard math" property works. see how → and why →.

about "permanent"

once a vote event is published to a public nostr relay, we cannot remove it. we don't own the relay. we have no admin call. we can't even ask politely — the protocol doesn't have a delete operation.

practically: as long as any one of the seven relays we publish to keeps the event, it's accessible. relays sometimes prune their own storage, but that's their decision, and we publish to multiple so loss requires coordinated pruning that doesn't happen.

this is a deliberate property of the architecture — see why → what "permanent" actually means for the rationale and the tradeoff.

what reaches the public ledger

because publication is permanent, we constrain what gets published rather than try to delete things after. the public ledger only accepts votes against entries in a pre-existing curated pool of ~34,000 public figures, brands, countries, and concepts. anything else stays on your device.

the pool comes from Wikipedia's top-pageview list, filtered for length and against the open LDNOOBW standard. it's our list; we maintain it; it doesn't grow on a vote.

what happens to a vote that isn't a pool entry:

  • obvious patterns (phone numbers, email addresses, SSN shapes, URLs, street addresses, credit-card-shaped digit runs) → private vote, stays on your device
  • anything else not in the pool → private vote, stays on your device

a private vote doesn't reach the public ledger, doesn't appear on the leaderboard, doesn't get sent to nostr or to our worker's database. the cooldown still applies (one vote per day) so the routing isn't a retry loophole. detail at how → moderation.

this is what we landed on after running into the realities of moderating a permanent public ledger as a hobby project. the journey is written up at why → what we wanted vs what we built.

your rights — and what they translate to here

GDPR + CCPA + similar laws give you specific rights. our architecture changes what each one means in practice:

standard rightwhat it means for blame.today
access (right to know what data we have) none personally. our worker has only SHA-256(your-pubkey) + the targets you voted on (which are public). the relays have the signed events (public). neither can be linked to you specifically without your private key.
rectification (correction) nothing to correct — we don't store anything about you that could be wrong. corrections to the public ledger (typos, etc.) work by voting again on the corrected form.
erasure ("right to be forgotten") we cannot delete from the public ledger. we can erase your locally-stored device key (delete the app) and our worker's cached row (email us). neither removes the signed events from public nostr relays.
portability your nostr keypair is yours — you can export the private key from the keychain and use it in any other nostr client.
object to processing stop using the app. there's no automated processing of personal data that continues without you.
withdraw consent same as above. no ongoing consent-required processing exists.
complain to a supervisory authority your local data-protection authority. in the EU, find yours at edpb.europa.eu.

children

the app isn't designed for children under 13 and we don't knowingly collect data from them. the App Store age rating reflects this. parents who discover their child is using the app can contact us and we'll help delete any identifiable cached data — though we can't remove anything already published to public nostr relays (see above).
