how this works

every vote is a signed nostr event. our app is one view of the public ledger. cloudflare is a cache. anyone can audit.
vote flow: iOS client signs vote with a per-device nostr keypair (BIP-340 Schnorr) ├─→ publishes signed event to 7 public nostr relays (the ledger) └─→ POSTs to cloudflare worker → D1 rollup (the cache) read path: this page → cloudflare worker → D1 → CDN-cached JSON (fast) auditors → any nostr relay → recompute from events (true) if the two ever disagree, the public ledger wins. the cache is opinion; the relays are the record.

tap a row → context

tap any entity on the leaderboard and the app shows a context sheet: a short blame summary, the headlines driving it this week, and a "more on the web" link for going deeper. headlines are aggregated from rss + text- only news sites (cnn lite, npr text, brutalist report, ddg lite) directly on your device — no centralized news middleman.

about the ai summaries

on apple-intelligence-capable devices (iPhone 15 Pro / 16 / 17, ipad m-series, iOS 26+), the blame summary is generated on your device from that week's headlines. nothing about your vote leaves your phone. on older devices the summary comes from a bundled snapshot or from peers' published content via nostr.
ai-written · may misstate · verify with the headlines.
every blame summary is written by an LLM (apple intelligence on your device, claude or llama in the bundle, peer-generated on nostr). LLMs can be confidently wrong. each headline carries its source domain and a date stamp — use those to fact-check.

cumulative coverage, deduped

headlines accumulate over a 7-day window per entity so you get a week of context, not just whatever happened in the last hour. near-duplicate titles ("trump signs tariff bill" vs "tariff bill signed by trump") are collapsed at merge time so a single hot-news event doesn't flush out diverse coverage from earlier in the week.
how we dedup, honestly: token-set overlap. we tokenize each headline (lowercase, drop stopwords + tokens under 3 chars + pure-numeric), then check if |A ∩ B| / min(|A|, |B|) ≥ 0.65. it catches reword-variants that share vocabulary ("tariff bill signed by trump" / "trump signs tariff bill"). it doesn't catch paraphrases with totally different words ("import duties become law" vs "tariff bill signed"). that's a known limitation — semantic dedup would collapse those but also risks collapsing genuinely-different stories that are topically adjacent, so we picked the safer failure mode. source: EntityRefreshCache.headlinesAreSimilar.

how trending works

the news is editorial. we're a calculator pointed at headlines. the cloudflare worker pulls news from ~10 free sources daily (yahoo, npr, the guardian, hacker news, reddit subs, polymarket, kalshi, wikipedia pageviews), extracts blameable entities from each headline via workers ai, weights each mention by source reach × headline negativity, and writes the top targets into the seeded leaderboard. users layer their votes on top.
formula: attention_units = Σ (source_weight × mention_count) + wikipedia_pageviews_today + Σ (market_volume) × calibration_factor avg_negativity = mean of per-headline negativity scores [0..3] (assigned by workers ai llama-3.2) seeded_count = round(0.001 × attention_units × avg_negativity) source weights (rough monthly-reach proxy): yahoo news 200,000 / mention npr top stories 100,000 the guardian 80,000 google news 50,000 (aggregator discount — items already deduped) hacker news 30,000 reddit r/news 30,000 reddit r/worldnews 30,000 reddit r/politics 25,000 (partisan, slight discount) reddit r/technology 20,000 wikipedia actual pageviews (sqrt-compressed above 1M) polymarket/kalshi market volume × 0.05 implementation: worker/src/index.ts NEWS_SOURCES + extractBlameTargets. full explainer + a worked example: blame.today/SEEDING.md
why is trump on top? because the news talks about him in negative tones a lot. multiple sources, daily, across categories (politics, courts, markets). high mention count × moderate-to-high negativity = high seeded_count. it's mechanical. if the news talks more about putin tomorrow, putin moves up. we don't hand-pick. SEEDING.md has the worked example.

verify the math

we don't ask you to trust us. here's exactly how to recompute today's leaderboard yourself from the public nostr ledger. if your numbers don't match ours, we're lying. call us out.
protocol: nostr NIP-01, kind 30078 (parameterized replaceable) filter: {"kinds":[30078], "#d":["blame:vote:<YYYY-MM-DD>"]} relays we publish to: relay.damus.io nos.lol relay.nostr.band relay.snort.social relay.primal.net offchain.pub nostr.mom steps: 1. connect to any relay above via WebSocket 2. send the filter (see /verify?day=<day> for the exact REQ) 3. for each EVENT, parse content as JSON → (pubkey, target) 4. group by target. unique pubkey count per target = real_count 5. compare to /leaderboard?day=<day> machine-readable: /verify?day=<today>

why this matters: we don't own the data — the protocol does. you don't have to trust the company, the company can't lie undetectably, and if we shut the worker off tomorrow the votes are still there. that's the property we want.
