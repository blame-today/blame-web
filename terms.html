the one rule that matters — one vote per device per day

the entire integrity of the leaderboard rests on this. Apple App Attest verifies your install is a real one; our worker records one signed vote per SHA-256(public_key) per UTC day. that's the system.

if you try to game it — multiple installs, jailbreak bypasses, replay attacks against the relays — we may permanently exclude your install from the public ledger without notice. we don't owe explanations for these decisions; the goal is preserving the property the architecture exists to defend (see why → what "permanent" means).

your votes — what you grant + why "permanent" matters here

when you tap VOTE, you grant us, the public nostr relays, and anyone who reads from them a perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free license to publish, copy, archive, and display the vote event (target id, target label, day, hashed install key, signature) as part of the public ledger.

this isn't a clause we're inventing — it's just stating what the architecture already makes true. the signed event is broadcast to seven public nostr relays we don't operate; once it's on those relays, we cannot retrieve it. relay operators don't owe you a delete operation.

if you don't want this, don't tap VOTE. there's no draft state, no editable post-publish view. the UI shows you the target before you commit and gives you a moment to back out.

moderation — pool-only public ledger

the public ledger only accepts votes against entries in a curated pool we maintain (~34,000 public figures, brands, countries, and concepts, sourced from Wikipedia top-pageviews and filtered against the open LDNOOBW list). a vote on anything else stays on your device only as a private vote.

we don't moderate by political viewpoint. the gate is mechanical: the target is either in the pool (or in the derived trending / cached sets) or it routes to private. the pool doesn't grow on a vote — it grows under our control. detail at privacy → what reaches the public ledger and how → moderation.

we may also remove a row from our worker's cached leaderboard rollup at our discretion (for legal compliance, spam, etc.). this does not remove the underlying events from the public nostr relays — we have no such power — but it does affect what shows up in our leaderboard view.

questions, concerns, or arguments about the pool live at github.com/blame-today/blame/issues (public, no ETA on response — it's a hobby project). for private concerns, hello@blame.today.
